State: Jammu & Kashmir

Jammu & Kashmir's traditional attire is characterized by the Phiran, a woolen gown worn by both men and women. Women's Phirans are often adorned with intricate embroidery, while men's attire is more simplistic. This traditional dress reflects the region's cultural heritage and provides warmth in the mountainous climate.

State: Goa

Goa's traditional attire is influenced by Portuguese and Indian cultures. Women often wear the Kunbi saree or the Pano Bhaju. Men typically wear the traditional attire called the Kashti.

State: Himachal Pradesh

Himachal Pradesh's traditional attire includes the Kurta, Dhoti, and Churidar for men, while women wear the Salwar Kameez and Ghagra Choli. The attire often reflects the colorful and vibrant culture of the region.
